The flagship of the Kit. Session to Song reads your Session clips and recreates
them in the Arrangement at the bars you lay out: real clips on real tracks,
named, colored, with named cue points marking each section.

## What it does

You map which Session scenes become which song sections and give each a length
in bars. The extension plans the section boundaries, then writes the clips into
the Arrangement. MIDI notes are copied; audio clips are referenced by file. The
whole build is one undo.

## Right-click path

Right-click a Scene (or use the general entry), then **Build Arrangement from
Session…**. A dialog opens with a small table: one row per section, each with a
name, a scene picker, a bar count, and a color. A free-text field also parses a
line like `Intro 8, Verse 16, Chorus 16, Bridge 8, Outro 8`. Press Build. A
reverse **Export Structure** reads existing cue points back to text.

## Honest beta limit

Session to Song reads your Session clips and recreates them in the Arrangement at
the bars you lay out. It copies MIDI notes and references audio clips by file,
plus clip name and color. It does not carry over every clip nuance: warp markers,
clip envelopes, and follow actions are not part of what the beta lets an
extension write, so complex warped or automated clips may need a touch-up after
the build. It assumes 4/4 unless a scene's time signature is read (the SDK has no
Set-level time signature; signature lives on the scene). Locators are written as
cue points (time plus name), not Live's colored section locators. User-invoked,
and the whole build is one undo.
